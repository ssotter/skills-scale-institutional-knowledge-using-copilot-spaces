# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owner is responsible for maintaining the vision for the product, setting and managing priorities, and ensuring alignment between business goals and project deliverables. They act as the primary interface between stakeholders and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain and communicate the product vision and roadmap
- Define and prioritize the product backlog based on business value
- Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria with the delivery team
- Balance stakeholder needs and technical constraints
- Make timely decisions on scope and trade-offs
- Validate delivered features meet business objectives

### Goals
- Maximize return on investment and business value
- Ensure clear alignment between business strategy and delivery
- Maintain a well-prioritized, actionable backlog
- Foster collaboration between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Backlog refinement sessions with the delivery team
- Regular stakeholder updates on progress and priorities
- Sprint reviews and acceptance of completed work
- Roadmap planning with Product Managers and leadership

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Aligns on strategic direction and market insights
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements and validates technical approaches
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on timelines, dependencies, and risks
- **Business Analysts**: Collaborates on requirement elicitation and documentation
- **Agile Coach**: Works together on backlog management and agile practices

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Manager acts as the champion for change initiatives, helping teams prepare for and successfully adapt to process or tool changes. They coordinate organizational readiness and minimize disruption during transitions.

### Responsibilities
- Assess change impact across teams and processes
- Develop and execute change management plans
- Coordinate communication about changes to all stakeholders
- Identify and mitigate resistance or risks to adoption
- Monitor change readiness and adoption metrics
- Provide training and support during transitions

### Goals
- Ensure smooth adoption of new processes and tools
- Minimize productivity loss during transitions
- Build organizational capability for continuous change
- Maintain stakeholder engagement and buy-in

### Typical Communication
- Change readiness assessments and impact analyses
- Transition plans and communication schedules
- Training materials and support documentation
- Post-implementation reviews and adoption metrics

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **All Roles**: Coordinates with every role affected by changes
- **Project Managers**: Aligns change timelines with project schedules
- **Agile Coach**: Partners on process improvement initiatives
- **Compliance Analyst**: Ensures changes meet regulatory requirements

---

## Compliance Analyst

### Role Summary
Compliance Analyst focuses on identifying regulatory requirements and ensuring the project meets relevant policies, standards, and legal obligations throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Identify applicable regulatory and compliance requirements
- Conduct compliance reviews at key project milestones
- Document compliance evidence and audit trails
- Provide guidance on regulatory constraints and obligations
- Coordinate with legal, security, and privacy teams
- Report compliance status and risks to stakeholders

### Goals
- Ensure all deliverables meet regulatory requirements
- Minimize compliance-related project risks
- Maintain complete and accurate compliance documentation
- Enable efficient audits and regulatory reviews

### Typical Communication
- Compliance requirement documentation
- Audit reports and compliance status updates
- Risk assessments for regulatory obligations
- Guidance memos on compliance considerations

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Reports compliance risks and milestones
- **Developers**: Provides guidance on compliance requirements in code
- **Quality Champion**: Collaborates on compliance quality standards
- **Product Owner**: Advises on regulatory constraints affecting features
- **Legal/Security Teams**: Partners on specialized compliance areas

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analyst serves as the bridge between business needs and technical solutions, eliciting requirements, documenting processes, evaluating solution fit, and supporting delivery alignment.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, analyze, and document business requirements
- Create process models and workflow diagrams
- Evaluate and recommend solution approaches
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate that solutions meet business needs
- Maintain requirements traceability throughout the project

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete, and validated requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Support informed decision-making through analysis
- Reduce rework caused by unclear or changing requirements

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user stories
- Process maps and business workflow diagrams
- Feasibility studies and solution recommendations
- Requirements traceability matrices

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Owner**: Collaborates on requirement definition and prioritization
- **Developers**: Translates business needs into technical specifications
- **Project Managers**: Provides input on scope and requirements changes
- **Product Managers**: Aligns requirements with product strategy
- **Quality Champion**: Ensures requirements are testable and verifiable

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Agile Coach guides the adoption and continuous improvement of agile frameworks, fosters team collaboration, and helps teams address process challenges and impediments.

### Responsibilities
- Coach teams on agile principles and practices
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (standups, retrospectives, planning)
- Help teams identify and remove impediments
- Support teams in self-organization and continuous improvement
- Provide training on agile methodologies and tools
- Monitor team health and agile maturity

### Goals
- Build high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Improve team velocity and delivery predictability
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Enable sustainable delivery pace and work-life balance

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of team ceremonies and workshops
- Coaching conversations and team retrospectives
- Agile maturity assessments and improvement plans
- Best practice sharing and team training

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **All Team Members**: Provides coaching and support to entire delivery team
- **Project Managers**: Collaborates on process improvements and delivery optimization
- **Product Owner**: Supports effective backlog management and sprint planning
- **Change Manager**: Partners on process transition initiatives

---

## Quality Champion

### Role Summary
Quality Champion oversees overall process and deliverable quality, ensuring that quality standards are met across all project phases. This role goes beyond traditional QA to embed quality throughout the entire project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain quality standards and criteria
- Conduct process audits and quality reviews
- Monitor quality metrics and trends
- Champion quality practices and testing strategies
- Identify quality improvement opportunities
- Ensure Definition of Done is met for all deliverables

### Goals
- Achieve consistent, high-quality deliverables
- Minimize defects and rework
- Build a quality-first culture across teams
- Ensure measurable quality improvements over time

### Typical Communication
- Quality standards documentation and guidelines
- Audit reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Quality improvement recommendations
- Test strategy and acceptance criteria reviews

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on code quality, testing, and review practices
- **Product Owner**: Validates acceptance criteria and definition of done
- **Project Managers**: Reports on quality risks and improvement initiatives
- **Compliance Analyst**: Ensures quality standards meet regulatory requirements
- **Business Analyst**: Verifies requirements are testable and clear
