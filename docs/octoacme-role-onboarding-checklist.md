# OctoAcme — Role Onboarding Checklist

## Purpose
Ensure new team members joining a project have clear understanding of their role, responsibilities, and how they interact with other roles on the team.

## When to use
Use this checklist when onboarding any new team member to an OctoAcme project, regardless of their role.

---

## General Onboarding (All Roles)

### Week 1: Orientation & Context
- [ ] Review project one-pager and understand problem statement
- [ ] Read project charter and success metrics
- [ ] Understand project timeline and current phase
- [ ] Review the [OctoAcme Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) document
- [ ] Identify your primary role and responsibilities
- [ ] Meet with Project Manager for project overview
- [ ] Get access to project repositories, boards, and communication channels
- [ ] Review existing documentation in docs/ and .copilot/ folders

### Week 2: Team Integration
- [ ] Attend all relevant team ceremonies (standups, planning, reviews)
- [ ] Meet one-on-one with key collaborators from other roles
- [ ] Review current sprint/iteration goals and backlog
- [ ] Understand escalation paths and decision-making process
- [ ] Review risk register and current project risks
- [ ] Identify your onboarding buddy or mentor

### Ongoing: Continuous Learning
- [ ] Participate in retrospectives and share feedback
- [ ] Update documentation based on learnings
- [ ] Ask questions in team channels
- [ ] Review project communication cadence and expectations

---

## Role-Specific Onboarding

### Product Owner
- [ ] Review product vision and roadmap with Product Manager
- [ ] Understand stakeholder landscape and expectations
- [ ] Review and prioritize current backlog items
- [ ] Establish backlog refinement cadence with team
- [ ] Review acceptance criteria standards
- [ ] Understand budget and resource constraints

### Developers
- [ ] Clone repository and set up development environment
- [ ] Review coding standards and PR conventions
- [ ] Run existing test suite successfully
- [ ] Review CI/CD pipeline and deployment process
- [ ] Complete first small task or bug fix
- [ ] Participate in code review (as reviewer and reviewee)

### Project Manager
- [ ] Review project governance and reporting requirements
- [ ] Understand stakeholder communication preferences
- [ ] Review current risks, dependencies, and blockers
- [ ] Set up regular sync cadences with team and stakeholders
- [ ] Review budget tracking and resource allocation
- [ ] Understand escalation triggers and protocols

### Product Manager
- [ ] Review market research and competitive landscape
- [ ] Understand user personas and customer needs
- [ ] Review product metrics and success criteria
- [ ] Align with stakeholders on priorities
- [ ] Review feature requests and feedback channels
- [ ] Understand roadmap dependencies

### Business Analyst
- [ ] Review existing requirements documentation
- [ ] Understand business process and workflows
- [ ] Meet with business stakeholders
- [ ] Review requirements traceability approach
- [ ] Understand tools for requirements management
- [ ] Identify upcoming requirement gathering needs

### Agile Coach
- [ ] Assess current team agile maturity
- [ ] Observe team ceremonies and dynamics
- [ ] Identify coaching opportunities and impediments
- [ ] Review team retrospective history
- [ ] Understand team's agile framework (Scrum, Kanban, etc.)
- [ ] Establish coaching relationship with team

### Quality Champion
- [ ] Review quality standards and definition of done
- [ ] Understand testing strategy and coverage
- [ ] Review quality metrics and trends
- [ ] Assess current quality processes and tools
- [ ] Identify quality improvement opportunities
- [ ] Review compliance and regulatory quality requirements

### Change Manager
- [ ] Understand upcoming changes and transitions
- [ ] Assess stakeholder change readiness
- [ ] Review change impact and affected groups
- [ ] Develop change communication plan
- [ ] Identify change champions and resistors
- [ ] Plan training and support activities

### Compliance Analyst
- [ ] Identify applicable regulations and standards
- [ ] Review existing compliance documentation
- [ ] Understand audit requirements and schedule
- [ ] Meet with legal and security teams
- [ ] Review compliance risks on risk register
- [ ] Establish compliance checkpoint cadence

## Tips for Success
- Don't hesitate to ask questions—everyone was new once
- Document what you learn to help future team members
- Seek feedback early and often
- Build relationships across the team
- Focus on understanding the "why" behind processes
